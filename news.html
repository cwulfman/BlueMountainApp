Blue Mountain Newsletter

2014-03-12

Grant Completed

In August 2014, Blue Mountain completed its inaugural NEH grant cycle. In that two-year period, we digitized and made accessible 2,441 magazine issues, comprising 56,466 pages and 60,073 articles.

New Content

This academic year – as we wait to hear back about a next NEH grant - we are adding several new titles to the Blue Mountain collection: Pan, Ver Sacrum, Apollon, and Vestik Rabotnikov Iskusstv. At the same time, we’ve been working on a parallel initiative, the German Modernism Project, which focuses on German language magazines from Princeton’s Blau Memorial Collection. Five titles from that collection - Die Schöne Raitatät, Genius, Der Gegner, Die Weissen Blatter, and Der Zweeman - will be included in the Blue Mountain Project archive this summer.

New Project Page

We are happy to announce the launch of the new Blue Mountain project page: http://bluemountain.princeton.edu. Developed to better meet the needs of researchers and to prepare for a large-scale expansion of services over the next year, the new project page provides a browsable catalog of Blue Mountain contents with links to full-text versions in the Blue Mountain Archive. This semester, Sophie Setia (PhD candidate in English, University of London) will help us compile bibliographic histories for Mask, 291, Coeur a barbe, Surrealisme, SIC, Poesia, and Veshch. That rich data will be integrated into the new project page and allow for even more powerful discovery of Blue Mountain content.

Expanded Advisory Board

A major development at Princeton this year was the establishment of the Center for Digital Humanities (CDH), a hub for DH work on campus. Blue Mountain is one of the sponsored CDH projects. We’re very pleased that Jean Bauer, Associate Director of the CDH, has joined the Blue Mountain Steering Committee. We also welcome Josh Kotin, Assistant Professor in Princeton’s Department of English, to our Board of Directors.

Co-Sponsored Event

Blue Mountain will co-sponsor a talk by Nicholas Sawicki (Art History, Lehigh) on “Avant-Garde Fissures in the Modern Czech Art Press: Traces in the Printed and the Digital,” the keynote for the “Decoding the Periodical” conference on Slavic Periodical Studies at Princeton on Friday, March 27. For more info see: http://seeps.princeton.edu.

New Blue Mountain Website

2014-03-06

Welcome to the new and improved Blue Mountain project page. This page contains all of the useful information found on the earlier version, such as who we are, the Project's mission and goals, and which titles are in our collection.

The most important new feature is the Catalog, which has been designed to enhance the browsing experience through the addition of high-resolution images from our collection. Readers can now get a snapshot of the Blue Mountain collection, a particular title, or a single issue not only by reading bibliographic information, but by seeing the vivid page images of our avant-garde magazines side-by-side.

As before, full-text searching of the collection is available at the Blue Mountain Archive (a site powered by the Veridian collection-management system), which also allows for readers to download issues of our magazines as PDFs.

Our Help page gives guidance about navigating this page and about some of the more technical sides of Blue Mountain.

Nicholas Sawicki to lecture on Czech Avant-Garde

2014-03-06

Nicholas Sawicki, the Frank Hook Assistant Professor of Art History at Lehigh University, will speak on “Avant-Garde Fissures in the Modern Czech Art Press: Traces in the Printed and the Digital,” on Friday March 27 at Princeton University.
