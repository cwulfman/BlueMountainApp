New Blue Mountain Website

2014-03-06

Welcome to the new and improved Blue Mountain project page. This page contains all of the useful information found on the earlier version, such as who we are, the Project's mission and goals, and which titles are in our collection.

The most important new feature is the Catalog, which has been designed to enhance the browsing experience through the addition of high-resolution images from our collection. Readers can now get a snapshot of the Blue Mountain collection, a particular title, or a single issue not only by reading bibliographic information, but by seeing the vivid page images of our avant-garde magazines side-by-side.

As before, full-text searching of the collection is available at the Blue Mountain Archive (a site powered by the Veridian collection-management system), which also allows for readers to download issues of our magazines as PDFs.

Our Help page gives guidance about navigating this page and about some of the more technical sides of Blue Mountain.

Watch this News page for announcements about Blue Mountain Project activities and events.

Nicholas Sawicki to lecture on Czech Avant-Garde

2014-03-06

Nicholas Sawicki, the Frank Hook Assistant Professor of Art History at Lehigh University, will speak on “Avant-Garde Fissures in the Modern Czech Art Press: Traces in the Printed and the Digital,” on Friday March 27 at Princeton University. Prof. Sawicki's talk is the keynote presentation at the Decoding the Periodical: A Workshop in Slavic, East European and Eurasian Periodical Studies conference. Click here for an abstract of the talk.
